About Myself

Amanda is a lawyer with strong interests in criminal justice, international law and public policy. She specialised in international human rights at Harvard Law School, where she developed an expertise in trafficking in persons and gender justice. She served as an expert at the UN Expert Group Meeting on the international legal definition of trafficking in persons in Nov 2016. She is also a published poet, her first collection "Professions" was shortlisted for the Singapore Literature Prize 2018. She co-founded ReadAble - a non-profit organisation that runs weekly English literacy classes for children and migrant women in a low-income neighbourhood.
